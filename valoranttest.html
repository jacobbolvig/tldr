“help sewers”
 

T1’s playing with A COACH. A MANAGER.

Illustration by Fuffy

It took all of about 10 minutes for Valorant to come back until we were able to get Twitter beef. And not just Twitter beef, but also a RIOT ruling? Let's talk about the TSM v T1 controversy.

Let’s set the scene: VCT Challengers 1 Open Qualifier. TSM v T1, both with new rosters for both teams coming out of the break. On Split, TSM was doing what they do best: lose. Then starts the fun.

Aleko, one of TSM’s new players tweets the following: “Forced on texas 7 ping 3 ping 6v5 all good we got this no problem.” Ok, teams get forced onto servers they don’t like playing on all the time, that's not weird.

But oh wait, 6v5? I’m fairly certain Valorant is generally a 5v5 game.

Aleko was talking about T1’s coach, dd8, who was found to be talking throughout the match to his team, in clear violation of VCT’s rules. 

This happened to be found out in the stupidest way by dd8 accidentally saying “help sewers” in all chat instead of team chat. We’ve all been there.

Subroza immediately started posting on Twitter about this, with a large majority of the Valorant community agreeing with him and wanting RIOT to enforce the rules.

A technical timeout was called while TSM was down 4-8 on Icebox, and then the game abruptly ended, with TSM players tweeting GG and T1 players tweeting, well, not GG.

RIOT later came out with a ruling stating the TSM has won the match by forfeit; T1 followed that with a suspension post regarding their coach. dd8 later came out with an apology, stating that it was mostly “encouraging/supportive statements and general reminders,” but that he believed in Riot’s completive integrity.

At this point, every former iBuyPower member has been on T1, so, realistically, why are we surprised?

The Buzz About Neon

Source Valorant

The Valorant world is lit up about the game’s newest character: Neon. From an absurdly catchy song in her reveal trailer to abilities that include going very fast, there’s a lot to love about the game’s sixth duelist.

However, when it comes to Neon I believe John Mayer said it best: “Who knows how long she can go before she burns away”.

Neon is everything that a PUG star could ask for, she has literally everything you need to frag out in ranked.

A stun to tilt someone playing a rat corner, a wall that lasts surprisingly long, a run and gun ultimate, and a Call of Duty slide.

The one thing that still leaves me uncertain is a lingering question: why would anyone pick her in pro?

In a game that is so focused on using utility in tandem with your teammates, Neon seems to add shockingly little to a team's game plan.

Before we get into the bad, let’s take a look at what she could add.

The Good

As we’ve already covered, she go fast. In terms of organized play, this can translate to a few things:

  • Fast flanks
  • Fast lurks
  • Fast lurks

Some teams may use her as a pocket pick to throw off an opponent's timings.

The slide portion of her signature ability can lead to very advantageous peeks; however, at a high level, this may or may not be a sustainable strategy.

I won’t sit here and say her kit is bad because it isn’t (we’re looking at you Yoru), but each of her abilities are useful in a selfish way.

Now I’m sure some of you are asking the question “Couldn’t you use the same argument against Reyna?”. Let’s compare the two on both sides of the spike.

The Bad

Attack: Both agents are built around entry fragging. In terms of purely getting the first kill of the round, Neon is probably better. Her ability to mix up how she peeks is pretty much unrivaled. 

  • If both agents can get that entry the advantage immediately goes to Reyna. Her ability to take 3 fights at full health and flash her team in is just so strong.

Defense: This is where I feel Neon really starts to fall apart. Besides rotation speed, she still doesn’t seem like the best choice.

  • Her ability to stall with concussions is good, but can be pushed through and just isn’t as good as Raze’s grenade.
  • She does not offer the same escapability as Reyna, Raze, Jett, or even Yoru, assuming Reyna can get a kill which, again, is not hard to imagine in pro play.
  • Her wall is better than Phoenix’s wall, but his ability to heal gives him the edge.

The Rest

Neon is an incredibly designed agent, remarkably fun to play, and basically a clip machine, but I think she’s too selfish for a team based game.

I’m sure she will get picked, we’ve seen TenZ play Yoru in grand finals, but with the addition of Chamber, and the upside of the other duelists, I think she will end up filling a niche role.
