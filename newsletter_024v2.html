A StarSeries from Brehze

 

An (Evil) Genius Move

Source: StarLadder / Igor Bezborodov

Somewhere, Nadeshot, the founder of 100Thieves, is crying to himself. "We could have had it all", he mumbles into his pillow, lying face down in his bed to hide the tears. "We could have had Ethan, CeRq, Brehze, Stanislaw and the other one. What's his name? The content guy?"

Unfortunately for 100T, they were gazumped on the signing of ex-NRG by Evil Geniuses, who have already now won two tournaments.

They took down Fnatic on two maps, coming back from a 0-9 deficit on Dust 2 to win 16-12, which is in impressive run, even if it is on Dust 2. Brehze went absolutely ballistic, dropping 28 frags in 28 rounds. He then dropped another 28 in just 25 rounds as EG breezed through Mirage 16-9, leaving the rifler on 56 kills on two maps. Yes, 56.

He didn't even get MVP - that went to Ethan, for his consistency throughout the tournament. We kind of get it, but 56 kills in two maps in the grand final is something else. That said, Ethan hasn't had an MVP in his career yet, so maybe we can find a way to be okay with it.

Furia ended up in third place, as they lost 2-1 in a ridiculously tight series with a 30 round map and a 34 round map to close it out. Half the players in that BO3 got less kills than Brehze did in 53 rounds in the grand final, by the way.

Brehze pulled off this absolutely insane 1v4 earlier in the tournament, too. Try telling him pistols are rng, we dare you.

From DH contenders to DH nontenders

 

Which way is North heading?

Talking about former North players, there might be five of them playing in North right now. Alright, that might be overreacting, but once tomorrow's update of HLTV's Top 30 list is coming out, North might very well drop out of the Top 20.

Not only is that their worst placement ever, it also doesn't look like they're improving. When they swapped k0nfig and cajunb for Kjaerbye and mertz in February 2018, they started playing DreamHack Opens to grind their way back to the highest tier. And that seemed to be the plan this time around as well, but losing the semifinal of the DH Winter qualifier with 0-2 to Copenhagen Flames, who are ranked 81st in the World, put a serious stop to that plan.

It's back to the drawing board for North, which started out as the 5th best team in the World, when they were founded in January 2017, but have only rarely ever reached those heights. Coincidentally MSL tweeted out that he's still looking for a team, just after North crashed out of StarSeries Season 8, and someone in the organization will have to wonder if bringing the former in-game leader back isn't the best course of action right now. Now what if you got back k0nfig, rubino and Magi... oh never mind.

They could always get HUNDEN, who won't be playing with Tricked tomorrow in a Danish derby against... oh, North. Well, North do need a miracle, and who else but a GOD can make miracles?
