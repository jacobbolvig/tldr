To franchise or not to franchise
 

ESL-Files

MTG.. oh wait, ESL announced their Pro Tour this week. That essentially means all their tier two tournaments - DreamHack Opens for example - actually feed into tier one tournaments, with the best ranking teams over the year making it into the Cologne and Katowice tournaments.

Amid rumours of a Valve sponsored franchise league, this was a much more palatable structure. It's not franchise, but it's not not-franchise either. Confused? So are we, but we're sure DeKay will clear it up for us down the line. But don't worry too much - there is just an overarching storyline between each tournament.

Seems like ESL want to be the real big dog in the tournament organiser fight - it has to be better than Best-of-one Pro Series taking over, though.

Back to the cave of Zeus
 

I am going to give up now I've won a motherf*ng major

There's plenty of things you can call Zeus, but a liar is not one of them. He didn't give up until he won a Major, and now he has a chance to go out with a second. Should be a pretty s1mple task.

DeKay's report suggests that Zeus' will go out with a BLAST (Pro Series Madrid), though he isn't sure if Zeus will retire or not.

Zeus, however, was far more laid-back, and claims he'll stay as long as Na'Vi keep winning in a YouTube interview on Na'Vi's channel - though this does assume his team WANTS to keep playing with him.

Now, it'd be nice if another team just cut their Russian-speaking IGL...

You raise me up...
 

I'm loving ANGE1 instead

HLTV might as well have tagged Na'Vi in the report titled 'HellRaisers to bench whole roster' (yes, really). When a Greek God dies, only an ANGE1 can replace him. Maybe. There have been rumours of Boombl4 taking over the reigns, but when an ANGE1 falls from heaven, you sit up and take notice.

It also means that former tier one players ISSAA and Oskar, are available. ISSAA has had something of a fall from grace in the new line-up, but a fresh start could do him wonders.

Tier one? In need of a fresh start? We might just know a guy.

Legal Deagles
 

Ninjas in Suits

Lawyer Ian Smith posted a long-form report on the NiP drama that started with Fifflaren accusing the Swedish org, and their CEO, Hichaim Chahine, of not paying players along with a host of other sketchy stuff.

Smith's conclusions do little to actually 'conclude' anything - he suggests much of the criticism is justified but also a lot of it isn't, as management has changed since. Good thing that WESA, which coincidentally has a bloke named Hicham Chahine as chairman, gave him 6 whole days to sift through 20,000 emails and access to only parts of the data...

Glad that's all cleared up.
