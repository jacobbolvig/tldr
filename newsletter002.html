As G2 showed yesterday, the French don't care when bombs go off. Investigative journalist numero uno, neL, dropped a bomb this afternoon. The too French; didn't read of it was neL announcing that FaZe and MIBR will enter negotiations after the Major.

Though there were rumours of FaZe having cashflow issues, this was slightly offset by coldzera offering to pay part of his own buyout. coldzera had mentioned himself that he was 'considering several offers', however, after waiting for most of them to die, he finally made a decision.

neL also mentions that Finnish heartthrob Aleksib may be replacing olofmeister if, or when, FaZe do badly at the Major.

Speaking of which, the family feud went the way of huNter-, who bowled over his cousin NiKo on Mirage. It was ottoNd who went nuclear, though, making him the second Finn to haunt them at a Major.

LIQUID EVAPORATING?
 

What else happened?

We shouldn't really be surprised that AVANGAR are good at the Major - after all, Jame has been Jaming saving strats for the Major the whole time. Liquid came into the Major as the favourite, but they need to unite the states of Liquid and solid if they want to end up winning.

Zeus is the only Major winner on NaVi, and if he keeps playing like he did today, it will stay that way. As much as we like the guy, 8 frags in a 36 round game really doesn't cut the mustard. On the mouz side, it was once again chrisJ with the ravenous bloodlust in the later stages of the game.

It wasn't a good day for the other zews, either. MIBR got absolutely obliterated by JaCkz, who dropped a 25-6 scoreline - dying only as often as zews killed an enemy. At least he has an excuse, though; TACO also got six kills, and he's not even a coach.

When allu plays, God flips a coin. Today, it landed on GOD allu. The Finnish sniper assassinated 55 targets in just 59 rounds - for reference, xseveN was second on the scoreboard with 36. ALEX, UK's finest, led Vitality with 38 tea-drinking Queen-saving frags, as his team fell to a 16-10 16-7 loss.

The Pokemon move Dream Eater only works on sleeping targets. Maybe if DreamEaters had played against NAF, they had a chance, but Renegades were the knight in diamond coin-coloured armour to defend our pick'ems' honour. Unless you had Renegades 0-3, of course.

North vs NiP was just... sad. The once-legendary name of NiP got dragged through the dirt, not for the first time this month. valde dropped a 40-16 scoreline as North crushed NiP 16-8 16-4 on Train and Dust II. Fifflaren, somewhere, has a pillow on his lap watching it.

In a game that, as far as we're aware, might still be going, NRG apparently beat Astralis 2-0. It took only 59 rounds for NRG to take out the Danes on Train, and they were so exhausted that they rolled over on Nuke 16-4. After losing in two maps worth of rounds, they must have thought they'd lost the first two maps.

Marvel at this
 

North vs America

Had you told us before the Major that North and Liquid were 1-2 after three games, we'd have... well, actually, believed you. After all, Liquid and Majors just always seem to end in tears, and North... well.. it's just never ez4aizy.

The match looks clear cut. On one side you have the World's undisputed #1 and a former Major winner. On the other, you have Team Liquid. All jokes aside, a North win would be insane. Ludicrous. ...they won't, will they?

North have a penchant for pulling off wins when they have no right to, and losses in similar circumstances. But Liquid have Captain America, and a North win is the exact type of injustice he's used to stamping out.

But here's the real dagger in North's spine - Liquid do not lose Mirage. North, on the other hand, do not win Mirage. Liquid permaban Train, which just so happens to be the one map North is actually winning consistently on, yikes.

Unless North have been secretly practicing Vertigo, they'll ban that. Even if they have, Liquid are yet to lose a game on it. Uh, good luck?
