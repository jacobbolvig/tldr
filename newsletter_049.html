The intro
 

ESL cuts ESL Pro League from 48 to 24 teams

If Thorin was bringing the fire, ESL brought the touch paper. They announced their list of invited teams (as seen below) and they have a bunch of the big boys, which made ol' Thorin look a bit silly for showing off about his (and B Site's) talent list. What use are great casters, if there's no great CS to cast?

Illustration by @NovaH_CS.

In fairness, ESL made themselves look a bit silly, too. There's a theme here.

They've announced invites, but only NiP have confirmed that they've said yes. It's not like they had much of a choice, being Sweden's 2nd best team and all. Not only that, ESL have annoyed plenty of people in the scene - not least the teams who thought they'd qualified for the Pro League via MDL, or simply staying in, but who have been mugged off by the new format.

We also won't be seeing Cloud9, MAD Lions, MiBR, CR4ZY, Dignitas and Gen.G, as Jacob Wolf confirmed they've signed with the B Site league.

Oh, so this is the WW3 everyone was talking about the other week.

But it wasn't for the best interests of '120+ people'. We should however expect a CSPPA statement any time. Or at least that's what chrisJ promised two days ago. What happened then
 

Thorin goes ballistic

Illustration by @NovaH_CS. Source photos: Dreamhack / Sebastian Ekman

Duncan spent more time on Twitter this weekend than those damned millennials your grandparents keep complaining about.

Thorin is the creative director for ESL Pro Tour's rival league, dubbed the B Site league. We mention this because it's extremely pertinent to the discussion. He has a vested interest in the success of it, which might be directly tied to whether or not EPT becomes a success.

So, reading the following, have in mind that he's not objective in this discussion.

It's like when Donald Trump insinuates that Joe Biden is a bit creepy. Yeah, he's not wrong, but there's an obvious ulterior motive. 

All that said, Thorin, as he often does, makes some great points. We'll shorten them for you, just imagine we're adding the words 'skrilla', 'context' and 'headshot machine' in there at different points for the authentic experience.

He called ESL's decision to take a 'team's earned league spot and give it to someone else' outrageous. He flamed the CSPPA for not helping the MDL teams out, and called for Valve to stop giving Majors to ESL.

He said he'll not work for ESL again as they 'treat people like valueless pawns in a sick game'. Now, what they did was shady, but it's not the newest Saw movie.

He later apologised to CSPPA and specifically chrisJ, who he'd earlier blamed for some of the perceived failings of the CSPPA, after admitting he didn't have any insight into exactly how those meetings went down.

That was most of the EPL stuff, but he found some time to subtweet neL, an infamous but mysterious French journalist who is usually pretty reliable, for his anonymity. neL isn't quite so anonymous any more, though, thanks to Ryan at Rush B Media.

Leipzig sees a German winner
 

Apparently there was some actual Counter-Strike

Three things in life are inevitable. Death, taxes and North flattering to deceive.

Even with big brain MSL at the wheel(chair), they threw away two match and series points on map two against Heroic, before getting shafted on map three, 16-2, and missing out on playoffs at DreamHack Open Leipzig.

They weren't the only Danish team coming away disappointed, though. Both Heroic and MAD Lions lost in the semi-finals, to BIG and Renegades respectively. We know 'rash B cyka', though.

"Wait, TLDR. Don't you mean Cloud9? There's no way they would lose out to the second best Australian team. Snapback-and-tanktop-wearing Americans told me they were good now!", we hear you cry. But you're wrong. Silly you.

They did lose out to Renegades after dexter took them out back and blew their brains out. Turns out dexter was a serial killer all along - we should have known from the name.

BIG came in as a team nobody was really talking about, but left on the tip of everyone's tongue. The home team took full advantage as they smashed Renegades in the final, 16-5/16-12 to take home the trophy.

More importantly, they have the bragging rights in Germany over Sprout, who are now indisputably the - sigh - wurst team in Germany.

k1to, who was most well-known for once going 0-17 in an actual, professional game, has come a long way since then. He ended up top of the scoreboard in the final with a 48-28 scoreline.

We haven't seen a turn-around this sharp since Virtus.pro went from a Major finalist to a truly dreadful team. Speaking of which...

Their new roster went from Major finalist to a pretty dreadful team. They got 11 rounds in 2 maps against Cloud9 in an elimination game. You could have been on NaVi, qikert.
